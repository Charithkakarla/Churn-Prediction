COMPANY POLICIES AND EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK

1. RETENTION STRATEGIES
Our company is committed to retaining top talent through various initiatives:
- Competitive compensation packages reviewed annually
- Performance-based bonuses and incentives
- Career development and training programs
- Work-life balance initiatives including flexible hours
- Employee wellness programs

2. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
Employees are evaluated quarterly based on:
- Project completion and quality
- Team collaboration
- Innovation and problem-solving
- Attendance and punctuality
- Professional development

Performance scores range from 1.0 (needs improvement) to 5.0 (exceptional).

3. PROMOTION POLICY
Employees are eligible for promotion after:
- Minimum 18 months in current role
- Consistent performance score of 4.0 or higher
- Completion of required training programs
- Manager recommendation

4. DEPARTMENT-SPECIFIC POLICIES

Sales Department:
- Monthly sales targets and commission structure
- Client relationship management requirements
- Quarterly team building activities

Engineering Department:
- Code review and quality standards
- Continuous learning budget ($2000/year)
- Innovation time (10% of work hours)

HR Department:
- Confidentiality and data protection protocols
- Employee relations and conflict resolution
- Recruitment and onboarding standards

Marketing Department:
- Campaign planning and execution guidelines
- Brand consistency requirements
- Cross-functional collaboration expectations

Operations Department:
- Process optimization initiatives
- Quality control standards
- Safety and compliance protocols

5. WORK-LIFE BALANCE
- Standard work week: 40 hours
- Flexible start times (7 AM - 10 AM)
- Remote work options (2 days/week for eligible roles)
- Paid time off: 15-25 days based on tenure
- Parental leave: 12 weeks paid

6. COMPENSATION STRUCTURE
Salary levels are determined by:
- Role and responsibility level
- Years of experience
- Performance history
- Market benchmarking
- Geographic location

Salary bands:
- Entry level: $35,000 - $50,000
- Mid level: $50,000 - $80,000
- Senior level: $80,000 - $120,000
- Leadership: $120,000+

7. EMPLOYEE DEVELOPMENT
- Annual training budget per employee
- Mentorship programs
- Leadership development tracks
- Technical certification support
- Conference attendance opportunities

8. EXIT INTERVIEW INSIGHTS
Common reasons for employee departure:
- Limited career growth opportunities
- Compensation below market rate
- Work-life balance concerns
- Lack of recognition
- Poor management relationships
- Relocation
- Better opportunities elsewhere

9. RETENTION RED FLAGS
HR monitors these indicators:
- Declining performance scores
- Decreased satisfaction levels
- Increased absenteeism
- Reduced participation in team activities
- No promotion in 3+ years
- Below-market compensation
- High workload (180+ hours/month)

10. INTERVENTION STRATEGIES
For at-risk employees:
- One-on-one meetings with management
- Career path discussions
- Compensation review
- Project reassignment
- Additional training opportunities
- Workload adjustment
- Recognition and appreciation initiatives
